7     
8        The AAT ``cidg`` table has almost the same structure as ``gidc``, 
9        just mapping CIDs to GlyphIDs instead of the reverse direction. 
10    
11       It is useful for fonts that may be used by a PDF renderer in lieu of 
12       a font reference with a known glyph collection but no subsetted 
13       glyphs.  For instance, a PDF can say “please use a font conforming 
14       to Adobe-Japan-1”; the ``cidg`` mapping is necessary if the font is, 
15       say, a TrueType font.  ``gidc`` is lossy for this purpose and is 
16       obsoleted by ``cidg``. 
17    
18       For example, the first font in ``/System/Library/Fonts/PingFang.ttc`` 
19       (which Apple ships pre-installed on MacOS 10.12.6) has a ``cidg`` table. 
20    
21       See also https://developer.apple.com/fonts/TrueType-Reference-Manual/RM06/Chap6gcid.html

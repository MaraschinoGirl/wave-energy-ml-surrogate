13   # Note that we use Y_DTYPE=float64 to avoid issues with floating point precision when
14   # summing gradients and hessians (both float32). Those are difficult to protect via
15   # tools like (Kahan-) Neumaier summation as in CPython, see
16   # https://github.com/python/cpython/issues/100425, or pairwise summation as numpy, see
17   # https://github.com/numpy/numpy/pull/3685, due to the way histograms are summed
18   # (number of additions per bin is not known in advance). See also comment in
19   # _subtract_histograms.
